Chapter 1: About the Author

I grew up in New York and have spent the majority of my life in the Charlotte, North Carolina area. Both places play a significant role for the person that I am today. Charlotte has grown considerably since I was young, and watching the city change has given me perspective on how quickly environments, and the skills required to operate within them, can shift.

My career has been rooted in IT since 2022, after switching paths from Psychology to Cybeersecurity and Software Development, with my current role as a Systems Support Administrator at Charter Communications in Charlotte. I work a Sunday through Wednesday shift inside a Systems Operations Center, where the bulk of my responsibilities consist of monitoring application systems and running P1/P2 outage calls. The SOC environment demands that you stay calm under ambiguity and develop judgment quickly, because the cost of a wrong call is measured in service outages and delayed resolution, not missed deadlines. That operational mindset; bias toward signal, skepticism toward noise, has become central to how I approach every problem I work on.

Outside of work, I have been a photographer since the age of five. It started as something personal and grew into a genuine craft over time. Photography taught me to read light, composition, and timing long before I ever thought about those same principles in a technical context. I also played college football as a defensive lineman, an experience that still informs how I think about pressure, leverage, and preparation.

My long-term career target is AI Security Engineering, a field where operational intuition from the SOC intersects directly with machine learning systems and threat detection. I hold an AWS Cloud Practitioner certification and am actively building a portfolio of projects that treat AI systems as a security surface. The goal is to exit my current role and step into an engineering position where the work is harder, the scope is wider, and the title reflects the actual job.
